Adoption services

Families can explore programs designed to help children find safe, permanent homes.

Family readiness

Orientation sessions, readiness checklists, and training that explain licensing, timelines, and expectations.

Child matching

Caseworker-guided matching based on age range, sibling placement needs, medical supports, and family strengths.

Placement transition

Visit planning, transition calendars, and coordination with foster families, therapists, and schools.

Post-adoption support

Referrals for counseling, crisis support, parent mentoring, and respite resources after finalization.

Family resources

Practical information for navigating paperwork, benefits, and community support.

Financial assistance

Learn about adoption subsidies, reimbursement for non-recurring costs, and Medicaid eligibility when applicable.

Education and training

Access webinars on trauma-informed parenting, attachment, school advocacy, and support for teens.

Legal and records help

Get help understanding consent, finalization steps, and how to request non-identifying records.

Youth-centered planning

Specialized planning for older youth, sibling groups, and children who need ongoing behavioral or medical support.

Frequently asked questions

Answers to common questions families may ask an agency assistant.

  1. Who can begin the adoption process? Adults age 21 or older who meet the agency’s screening and training requirements can apply.
  2. How long does the process take? Timelines vary, but orientation, training, and home study preparation often take several months.
  3. Can we adopt siblings? Yes. The agency prioritizes keeping siblings together whenever it is safe and appropriate.
  4. Is support available after finalization? Yes. Many families continue to receive referrals for counseling, support groups, and crisis services.
  5. Are there services for teens? Yes. Teen-focused supports may include independent living planning, school coordination, and mentoring.
